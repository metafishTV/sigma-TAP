## Integration Points

- **TAP equation as sigma-TAP's core**: The constant-α (Eq. 26) and power-law (Eq. 28) forms from this paper are the equations sigma-TAP extends. The key extension: sigma-TAP adds σ(Xi) feedback to the α parameter, making growth rate endogenous rather than exogenous. The paper's Eq. 25 (general form with $\alpha_i$ coupling constants) is the most general statement of what sigma-TAP modifies — each $\alpha_i$ becomes $\alpha_i \cdot f(\sigma, \text{trust}, \text{topology})$.
  - **Candidate forward notes**: The §5.1–§5.3 design notes should reference this paper as the definitive source for the unmodified TAP equation that sigma-TAP extends.

- **Opacity principle = cross-metathesis novelty mechanism**: The completeness relation (Eq. 24) formalizes why sigma-TAP treats novel cross-metathesis products as first-class entities. Composites and their components function as independent basis states in the effective Hilbert space. This is the formal justification for sigma-TAP's type_set architecture: a composite type {a,b,c} entering the shared type_set is independent of its constituent types a, b, c.
  - **Concept map entries**: `Cortes:opacity_principle` maps to cross-metathesis novelty mechanism / type_set architecture.
  - **Candidate forward notes**: The completeness relation should be cited in §5.18 (cross-metathesis design) as the formal basis for treating novel types as independent DOF.

- **Ever-expanding config space = the philosophical foundation**: The paper's central philosophical argument — biology breaks the Newtonian paradigm because its configuration space is ever-expanding — IS the claim sigma-TAP computationally instantiates. Every simulation timestep that generates novel types via cross-metathesis is an expansion of config space. The paper provides the strongest theoretical grounding for sigma-TAP's core design choice.
  - **Concept map entries**: `Cortes:ever_expanding_config_space` maps to type_set space growth / core sigma-TAP mechanism.

- **Non-ergodicity + sigma feedback**: The paper's non-ergodicity argument ($20^{200}$ possible chains, almost none actualized) explains WHY sigma feedback matters: since agents cannot explore all of type_set space, the path they take through it is critically important. σ(Xi) guides which adjacent possibilities are actually explored — it is the agent-level mechanism that navigates a non-ergodic landscape. Without sigma feedback, the agent wanders randomly through an impossibly vast space; with it, exploration is directed by accumulated experience.
  - **Candidate forward notes**: Non-ergodicity should be referenced in the sigma feedback design rationale — it provides the information-theoretic justification for why modulated exploration outperforms random exploration.

- **Hockey-stick phase transition as simulation diagnostic**: The paper's central empirical result (Figure 1) shows characteristic hockey-stick behavior: ~800 timesteps of near-flat growth, then explosive upturn. sigma-TAP simulations should exhibit analogous dynamics. The transition timestep depends on parameters — sigma feedback modulates when it occurs. This provides a diagnostic: if sigma-TAP simulations do NOT show hockey-stick behavior under appropriate parameters, something is structurally wrong.
  - **Candidate forward notes**: Hockey-stick onset timestep as a function of σ strength could be a Stage 3C+ diagnostic target.

- **Creative potential Q as simulation diagnostic**: Q = information created during evolution not present in initial conditions. Operationalizable for sigma-TAP as: $Q(t) = |\text{type\_set}(t) \setminus \text{type\_set}(0)| / |\text{type\_set}(0)|$. A Newtonian paradigm simulation (no genuine novelty) would have $Q = 0$; a TAP-active sigma-TAP simulation should have $Q \gg 1$. Could serve as a macro-level diagnostic alongside Youn ratio.

## Open Questions

1. **Scale bridging**: The paper works at cosmological/molecular scale (CHNOPS, $10^{10^{237}}$ microstates). sigma-TAP works at agent/social scale (10-100 agents, type_sets of ~6-20 types). The structural TAP form is preserved, but the quantitative regime is radically different. Does the TAP mechanism behave the same way at social scale as at biochemical scale? The power-law suppression parameter ($a = 250$ biological vs. $a = 3$–$8$ social) suggests different combinatorial physics, but the hockey-stick signature should be scale-invariant.

2. **Template synthesis analog**: What is sigma-TAP's equivalent of template synthesis — the transition from pre-TAP to open-ended evolution? Is it the formation of the first family group (enabling sustained cross-metathesis)? The first novel type that itself participates in further combination? The sigma-TAP simulations should exhibit an identifiable phase transition; locating it would confirm that the TAP mechanism is functioning as theoretically expected.

3. **Alive/Dead boundary**: The paper uses a binary macroscopic observable (Alive/Dead). sigma-TAP's temporal state machine has 5 states (expanding, established, declining, inertial, recovering) + state 0 (disintegrated). The relationship between the binary Alive/Dead and the 5-state temporal machine needs specification: is Dead = state 0 only? Or does prolonged inertial state also count? The paper's definition (≥1 living organism) suggests Dead = complete absence of TAP-active dynamics.

4. **Opacity and trust**: The opacity principle says composites "forget" their origin. But sigma-TAP's trust metrics (τ_pair, τ_group) REMEMBER interaction history. There is a productive tension: opacity enables combinatorial novelty (forget origin → recombine freely), while trust constrains which combinations actually occur (remember history → gate interactions). How do these two principles interact? Opacity may operate at the type level (types forget origin) while trust operates at the agent level (agents remember history). The distinction between type-level opacity and agent-level memory may be fundamental.

5. **Biological dark energy and L22**: The paper's most speculative claim — that biological information may modify vacuum energy — resonates with sigma-TAP's L22 channel (environment-environment dynamics operating independently of agents). If biological information genuinely contributes to cosmic entropy, then L22 dynamics are not just organizational metaphor but may connect to fundamental physics. This is too speculative for current implementation but worth noting as a long-term conceptual horizon.
