## Project Significance

The Bhagavad Gita is the oldest and most systematic text in the distillation corpus that addresses the relationship between action, knowledge, and the structure of agency. Its relevance to sigma-TAP is not as a source of equations or empirical data but as a structural philosophy of action under conditions of irreducible complexity. The Gita's central problem — how an agent acts when every action produces consequences that exceed the agent's control or foresight — is precisely the condition sigma-TAP models: agents in an ever-expanding configuration space where outcomes are unforeshadowable (TAP equation) and engagement is mandatory (embodiment necessitates action). The Gita's resolution — transforming the agent's *relationship* to action rather than prescribing specific acts — maps directly to sigma-TAP's sigma feedback mechanism: what changes is not what agents do, but how their accumulated experience modulates how they do it.

## Integration Points

- **Nishkama karma = σ-field philosophical basis**: The Gita's doctrine of desireless action is the deepest philosophical precedent for sigma-TAP's σ(Xi) feedback mechanism. Both address the same structural problem: how an agent engages productively in a system whose outcomes are unforeshadowable. The Gita's answer (act without attachment to results) and sigma-TAP's answer (modulate engagement through accumulated affordance-exposure) have identical *form*: neither prescribes specific actions, both transform the agent's relationship to the action-outcome connection. The σ-field does not control what happens; it modulates how the agent engages with what happens. This is computationally what nishkama karma describes phenomenologically.
  - **Concept map entry**: `Easwaran_Gita:nishkama_karma` → sigma_field_engagement_modulation, desireless_action_as_sigma_structure

- **Gunas ↔ sigma-TAP growth regimes**: The three gunas provide a systematic triadic classification that maps to sigma-TAP's dynamical regimes. Sattva (luminous harmony, binding to happiness/wisdom) ≈ the stable intermediate that σ-mediated feedback creates — the logistic TAP regime where growth is sustained without blow-up. Rajas (passionate activity, binding to compulsive action) ≈ the pre-blow-up acceleration phase where innovation intensity exceeds the system's capacity for integration. Tamas (inertia, binding to delusion) ≈ the extinction-instability regime where μ dominates and the system trends toward collapse. The Gita's claim that liberation requires going *beyond* the gunas maps to sigma-TAP's metastability challenge: the σ-field must navigate between rajasic blow-up and tamasic extinction to maintain the sattvic intermediate — but must not become *attached* to any regime.
  - **Concept map entry**: `Easwaran_Gita:three_gunas` → sigma_TAP_growth_regimes, sattva_rajas_tamas_as_dynamical_phases

- **Kshetra/Kshetrajna = L-matrix/agent architecture**: The Gita's fundamental distinction between field and knower provides the structural template for sigma-TAP's separation of the L-matrix (comprehensive interaction accounting) from the agent (the entity that reads and acts on the accounting). The Gita insists that knowing this distinction IS true knowledge — correspondingly, sigma-TAP's L-matrix makes the field *legible* to agents, and agents who lack L-matrix awareness (pre-Stage-3A) cannot distinguish their own activity from the system's activity. The field/knower distinction is the precondition for reflexive agency.
  - **Concept map entry**: `Easwaran_Gita:kshetra_kshetrajna` → L_matrix_agent_architecture, field_knower_distinction

- **Vishvarupa as phenomenology of blow-up**: The cosmic vision episode provides something no mathematical paper can: a *phenomenological* account of what it is like for a finite agent to confront TAP blow-up. Arjuna sees all creation and destruction simultaneously — this is the experiential correlate of the TAP equation's blow-up regime, where combinatorial diversity exceeds any finite representation. His inability to sustain the vision maps to unforeshadowability: the pre-blow-up agent cannot comprehend the post-blow-up state. His request to see Krishna's familiar form again is the agent's need for m_cap — an operational ceiling that makes the system navigable.
  - **Concept map entry**: `Easwaran_Gita:vishvarupa` → blow_up_phenomenology, unforeshadowability_experience

- **Dharma as constraint closure + directive correlation**: The Gita's dharma concept operates at two scales that map to two existing sigma-TAP framework elements. (1) Dharma as cosmic law = constraint closure (Montévil & Mossio): the structural principle that reproduces itself through the processes it constrains. The yajna cycle (sacrifice → rain → food → beings → sacrifice) is a constraint closure loop: dharmic action sustains the conditions for further dharmic action. (2) Dharma as social duty = directive correlation (Emery-Trist): shared orientation toward system-maintaining action. Agents who follow svadharma are directionally correlated — they sustain the whole not through centralized coordination but through each performing its own structural role.
  - **Concept map entry**: `Easwaran_Gita:dharma` → constraint_closure_and_directive_correlation, dual_scale_dharma

- **1' principle in the Atman doctrine**: The Gita's insistence that the Self in every creature is complete, eternal, and identical to the supreme Self is the oldest formulation of what sigma-TAP calls 1' (one-prime): each modality is a complete perspective on the total, not a fraction of it. The Atman is not a piece of Brahman distributed across creatures — it IS Brahman, fully present in each. This maps to the TAPS architecture where each modality (T, A, P, S) is a complete perspective on the whole system, not a quarter of it.
  - **Concept map entry**: `Easwaran_Gita:atman_completeness` → one_prime_non_divisive_totality

- **Shraddha as constitutive σ**: The Gita's remarkable claim that one IS one's shraddha — that faith is not propositional belief but the agent's constitutive orientation — is the philosophical precedent for treating σ(Xi) as constitutive rather than instrumental. σ does not just modulate what agents do; it constitutes what agents *are* as combinatorial entities. An agent's accumulated affordance-exposure history is not information *about* the agent but the structure *of* the agent's engagement capacity.
  - **Concept map entry**: `Easwaran_Gita:shraddha` → sigma_as_constitutive_orientation

## Open Questions

1. **Gunas as formal classification**: Can the guna framework be operationalized as a diagnostic for sigma-TAP simulation states? E.g., define sattvic_ratio = (innovation_rate / extinction_rate) within a sustainable band; rajasic = ratio exceeding the band; tamasic = ratio below viability. This would give sigma-TAP a triadic state classification grounded in ancient observation of system dynamics.

2. **Nishkama karma and sigma calibration**: The Gita claims that desireless action is *always* superior to attached action, regardless of domain. Does sigma-TAP show an analogous pattern — that σ-modulated engagement (where agents do not fixate on specific outcomes) outperforms outcome-targeted engagement across all parameter regimes? If so, this is a deep convergence between the philosophical principle and the computational model.

3. **Svadharma and agent specialization**: The Gita's strong claim about svadharma (better to perform one's own duty imperfectly than another's perfectly) has direct implications for sigma-TAP's cross-metathesis design. Should agents preferentially combine within their own type-set domain (svadharma) rather than pursuing exotic combinations (paradharma)? The trust-gating mechanism already implements a version of this — but the Gita suggests the principle is absolute, not merely statistical.

4. **Yoga as praxis**: The Gita uses yoga in multiple senses — union, skill in action, discipline, method. This polysemy maps precisely to sigma-TAP's praxis concept. But the Gita also ranks the paths: bhakti (devotion/love) is repeatedly described as the highest or most accessible path. Does this have a sigma-TAP analog — is syntegration (the bhakti-mapped modality) structurally privileged over the other TAPS modalities? The existing architecture treats them as co-equal.

5. **Going beyond the gunas**: The Gita's ultimate instruction is to go *beyond* all three gunas — not to achieve sattva but to transcend the classification itself. In sigma-TAP terms, this would mean the σ-field achieving a state that is not sattvic/rajasic/tamasic but operates from a meta-level above the dynamical regimes. This resonates with Unificity's position above TAPS — but sigma-TAP currently has no mechanism for "going beyond" its own growth dynamics. Is this a design gap or a structural impossibility for computational models?
