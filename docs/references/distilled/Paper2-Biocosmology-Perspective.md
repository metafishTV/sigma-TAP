# Cortês, Kauffman, Liddle & Smolin (2022) — Biocosmology: Biology from a Cosmological Perspective

**Source**: Marina Cortês, Stuart A. Kauffman, Andrew R. Liddle, and Lee Smolin, "Biocosmology: Biology from a cosmological perspective," April 20, 2022. Second in the Biocosmology series. 28 pages.

**Core contribution**: Introduces a three-type classification of cosmological statistical systems (Type I: ergodic/equilibrium, Type II: Hubble-timescale equilibration, Type III: permanently non-ergodic/living), argues that Type III systems require functional explanations alongside reductionist ones, proposes a fourth law of thermodynamics for Type III systems, and provides a formal definition of life as a Kantian Whole within a Type III system.

---

## Three Types of Cosmological Statistical Systems (Section 2)

Classification by thermalization time $t_{\text{thermal}}$ relative to Hubble time $t_H \approx 14.5$ Gyr:

### Type I: Ergodic Equilibrium Systems

- $t_{\text{equil}} \ll t_H$
- Satisfy Boltzmann's ergodic hypothesis: time averages equal ensemble averages
- Standard equilibrium thermodynamics applies
- Example: the early Universe (high-temperature radiation-dominated plasma)
- No coupling to cosmological degrees of freedom

### Type II: Hubble-Timescale Equilibration

- $t_{\text{eq-con}} \gg t_H$ (equilibration subject to constraints takes longer than Hubble time)
- Will eventually reach (possibly metastable) equilibrium, but not within cosmological time
- Examples: most stars and galaxies (nuclear-burning feedback loops drastically slow approach to equilibrium), white dwarfs, dissipative systems (Prigogine/Nicolis), autocatalytic networks (Kauffman), Turing pattern formation

**Key mechanism**: Stars have negative specific heat (gravitationally-bound systems heat up when energy is removed). Combined with the landscape of hundreds of stable nuclear bound states and MeV-scale barriers, this creates a complex web of metastable states that sequester nuclear potential energy for timescales far exceeding naive nuclear expectations.

**Metastable equilibrium**: When $t_{\text{eq-con}} \ll t_{\text{constraints}}$, the system achieves effective equilibrium on the restricted state space. White dwarfs exemplify this — supported by electron degeneracy pressure, they persist essentially indefinitely unless disrupted by external accretion.

### Resolving the BBN Paradox

The early Universe was in equilibrium, then cosmic nucleosynthesis (BBN) appears to spontaneously move it far from equilibrium — seemingly violating the second law.

**Resolution**: The pre-BBN equilibrium was a *false equilibrium* — equilibrium of the high-energy regime only, excluding nuclear bound states that couldn't exist at high temperature. When $T$ drops below $T_{\text{nucleo}}$, new stable states are "unveiled" (not created — they were always in the full Hilbert space but unoccupied). The maximum attainable entropy ceiling rises dramatically.

**Critical principle**: *Entropy is not an observable*. It is a function of the observer's knowledge of the system, dependent on which interactions are included in the calculation. The evolving cosmological entropy estimates ($S_{\text{relativistic}} \sim 10^{90}$ → $S_{BH} \sim 10^{104}$ → $S_\Lambda \sim 10^{124}$) demonstrate this — each revision came from recognizing previously-neglected interactions.

The distinction between "true" and "false" equilibrium mirrors true/false vacuum in field theory. False equilibrium is metastable; it evolves toward true equilibrium once constraints permit.

### Type III: Permanently Non-Ergodic Systems

- **No thermalization time exists** while the system is alive and energy flows persist
- State spaces are so vast that the Universe in its entire spacetime volume cannot populate more than a tiny fraction
- Radically non-ergodic: time averages ≠ ensemble averages, *forever*
- Examples: biological systems (currently the *only* known Type III systems)

**How Type III arises**: When the Universe cools below $T_{\text{mol}}$ (molecular bound states become stable), the landscape of possible molecular states explodes. The number of possible proteins alone ($20^{1000}$) vastly exceeds the number of baryons in the observable Universe ($\sim 10^{80}$) and even the spacetime volume in Planck units ($\sim 10^{120}$).

**Existence is rare**: In Type III systems, the question that must be asked about any organism or molecule is: why does *this one* exist while vastly more alternatives do not?

**Second law not violated**: The system is sustained by a slow steady flow of low-entropy energy (e.g., from the Sun). If the energy source ceases, the Type III system dies and becomes Type I or II. Including the energy source in the total system, entropy is always increasing.

**Potential longevity**: Type III systems could survive well beyond the stellar era ($\sim 10^{13}$ years) by exploiting stored nuclear potential energy in white dwarf remnants. A sufficiently intelligent Type III system might survive indefinitely.

## Functional Explanations (Sections 3–4)

### The Failure of Determinism in Type III

Consider all possible proteins of length ≤1000 amino acids: $N_P = 20^{1000}$ possibilities. Only ~$10^9$ are realized at any time — a vanishingly small fraction. The proteins that exist have almost identical physical properties to those that don't. How to explain the difference?

**Why determinism fails**: Extraordinary sensitivity to initial conditions. Running evolution again from even infinitesimally different initial conditions produces a completely different collection of creatures. Five mass extinctions demonstrate this — each followed by radically different species proliferating. Additionally: radioactive/cosmic-ray mutations are quantum-random; digital computation accumulates uncontrollable errors; no computer fitting in the Universe could integrate the biosphere's initial conditions forward.

### The Need for Functional Explanations

Purely reductionist explanations ("bottom up" from initial conditions and laws) cannot sufficiently explain why certain proteins/organisms exist while almost all others don't. What's needed: *functional* explanations.

**Functional explanation**: X exists because X contributes to the survival or well-being of a larger system S. What X does for S is its function.

**Two-part sufficient reason** for protein P's existence:
1. P is in the class of molecules that can, by the laws of physics, be made by a cell (reductionist, bottom-up)
2. P codes for an enzyme that catalyzes a reaction contributing to the survival/fitness of a larger organism (functional, top-down)

Both are necessary. Neither alone suffices.

### Kantian Wholes

The formal structure of functional explanation:

**Definition**: In a Kantian Whole, the Parts exist in the Universe *for and by means of* the Whole. All living organisms are Kantian Wholes.

Formal specification:
1. Occupied states contain interconnected bundles of subprocesses $K$, with outputs feeding into inputs of other subprocesses
2. Subprocesses perform tasks (requiring chemical/physical work) and functions (contributing to fitness of Whole $K$)
3. $K$ is a Kantian Whole when many of its subprocesses $P_I$ perform functions that increase the fitness of $K$
4. There is a symbiosis among subprocesses — they exist because their combined contributions make $K$ viable
5. Kantian Wholes can be arranged hierarchically; a subprocess may belong to multiple Kantian Wholes

**Downward causation**: The heart exists *because the cat exists* (not merely because of bottom-up physics). Functional explanations rely on downward causation. In biology, upward and downward causation are both present and necessarily so. This is compatible with physicalism — functional explanations work hand in hand with reductionist ones.

### Recursive and Excursive Properties

Type III systems are *recursive*: they have a code providing complete mapping from nucleic acid chains to catalytic functionalities, allowing the organism to store information about its own structure and reconstruct itself.

Type III systems are *excursive*: the same coding that gives information for self-construction can be altered to code any of a vast space of alternatives. The organism refers explicitly to itself (recursive) and implicitly to a vast set of adjacent possibilities (excursive).

### Three Arrows of Dynamics

1. **Type I arrow**: System returns to equilibrium; arrow ceases when equilibrium reached; time-reversal symmetric
2. **Type II arrow**: Extends from Big Bang to at least $10^3 \times t_H$ (stellar era); coupled to cosmological evolution
3. **Type III arrow**: Never reaches equilibrium while energy source persists; continues indefinitely; fundamentally irreversible

## Fourth Law of Thermodynamics (Section 5)

For a Type III system $\mathcal{T}$:
- $F_A$ = number of distinct actual functions being used at time $t$
- $F_P$ = number of possible functions (presently unexpressed codons, or achievable through few mutations)
- $R = F_P / F_A$

**Proposed fourth law**: For any Type III system, as long as non-equilibrium conditions persist:

$$F_P, \; F_A, \; \text{and} \; R \text{ all have a tendency to increase.}$$

This is NOT a violation of the second law — including the energy source, total entropy always increases. Type III systems are exploring low-entropy pockets of phase space, sustained by almost unlimited low-entropy energy from their source (e.g., Sun).

The ratio $R$ increases at the extraordinary rate described by tetration — the number of possible functions grows super-exponentially faster than the number of actual functions.

## Definition of Life (Section 6)

**A living organism is a Kantian Whole within a Type III system that is a non-equilibrium self-reproducing system with a metabolism, an identity and a boundary, and which is capable of open-ended evolution by heritable variation and selection or drift.**

Key elements of this definition:
- **Kantian Whole**: Parts exist for and by means of the Whole; functional explanations necessary
- **Type III**: Non-ergodic; existence is rare; the system never reaches equilibrium
- **Non-equilibrium self-reproducing**: Requires slow steady energy flow
- **Metabolism**: Cycles of energy and materials, controlled by feedback
- **Identity and boundary**: Controlling exchanges of materials, ion concentrations — each function requires work
- **Open-ended evolution**: Capable of constructing boundary conditions new to the Universe

**Work and constraints**: Work = constrained release of energy into few degrees of freedom. In a constraint-closed system, constraints constrain energy release in non-equilibrium processes that construct the very same set of constraints. Cells construct themselves. By heritable variation and natural selection, cells evolve to construct boundary conditions new to the Universe, creating new phase spaces and new possibilities.

## Key Concepts for sigma-TAP Integration

1. **Type III system = sigma-TAP's target phenomenon**: The simulation models agents that never reach equilibrium, where existence is rare, and whose state space grows faster than it can be explored. The MetatheticEnsemble is a Type III system in miniature.

2. **Functional explanation maps to TAPS signatures**: Why does *this* agent exist while others don't? Because its TAPS signature (function within the ensemble) contributes to ensemble viability. The TAPS signature is the functional explanation for an agent's persistence.

3. **Kantian Whole = the ensemble/group as self-sustaining system**: Agents exist for and by means of the ensemble (downward causation). The ensemble exists because of agents' contributions (upward causation). Both directions of causation are present and necessary.

4. **$R = F_P / F_A$ as measurable quantity**: The ratio of possible to actual functions increases over time. In sigma-TAP, this maps to the ratio of possible type combinations to actually instantiated type_sets — the adjacent possible growing faster than exploration.

5. **Recursive + excursive = self-metathesis + cross-metathesis**: The organism's self-referential coding (recursive) maps to L11/self-metathesis. The implicit reference to vast alternatives (excursive) maps to L12/cross-metathesis — opening adjacent possibilities.

6. **Three arrows = three temporal regimes**: The Type III arrow (never reaching equilibrium while energy flows) maps directly to the praxitive deferral model — agents persist as long as they defer consummation, sustained by environmental energy flow.

7. **Constraint closure = the conservation law**: Work constructs constraints that constrain energy release that produces work. This self-constructing cycle maps to the conservation law: minimal praxis for maximal syntegration — the variational principle selecting for self-sustaining cycles.

8. **Existence is rare → explains agent death**: In Type III systems, simply existing is a very rare property. Most possible configurations never get realized. Agent death in sigma-TAP is not exceptional but the default — persistence is what requires explanation.

9. **False equilibrium → apparent stability masks deeper dynamics**: Systems may appear stable (false equilibrium) while ignoring interactions that, once "switched on," reveal they are far from true equilibrium. Maps to the dormant/established states in the temporal machine — agents that appear stable may be in false equilibrium that breaks when new interactions (cross-metathesis) become available.
