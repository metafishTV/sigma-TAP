# Cortês, Kauffman, Liddle & Smolin (2022) — Biocosmology: Towards the Birth of a New Science

**Source**: Marina Cortês, Stuart A. Kauffman, Andrew R. Liddle, and Lee Smolin, "Biocosmology: Towards the birth of a new science," April 20, 2022. Perimeter Institute / Instituto de Astrofísica / Institute for Systems Biology. 35 pages. First in a series; companion papers are Paper 2 (Ref. [35]) and the TAP equation paper (Ref. [55]).

**Core contribution**: Argues that the configuration space of living systems is ever-expanding (unlike physics where Hilbert space is fixed), that emergent biological degrees of freedom must be counted as fundamental, and that the TAP equation models this expansion. Demonstrates that the number of biological microstates ($N_{\text{Bio}}$) can exceed the cosmological entropy bound ($N_\Lambda = \exp(10^{124})$), potentially making living biospheres the dominant source of information in the Universe.

---

## The Entropy Inventory (Sections 1–2)

The paper begins from cosmology's problem of initial conditions: explaining how unlikely our Universe is requires knowing its current entropy $S$, which via Boltzmann's formula gives the number of microstates and reveals the "price to be paid" at the Big Bang.

**Three contributions to cosmic entropy** (progressive discovery):

1. **Material content** (thermal particles): $S_{\text{relativistic}} \approx 10^{90}$ — dominated by CMB photons and neutrinos. Gives $\exp(10^{90})$ possible configurations. But this alone would yield only a cold plasma, not the cosmic web.

2. **Black holes**: Bekenstein-Hawking entropy $S_{BH} = (4\pi M^2)$, dominated by supermassive BHs ($\sim 10^9$ solar masses). Egan & Lineweaver: $S_{BH} = 3 \times 10^{104}$. This completely dominates particle entropy.

3. **Vacuum energy** (dark energy $\Lambda$): Bekenstein-Hawking area formula applied to cosmic event horizon: $S_\Lambda \approx 3 \times 10^{122}$. Corresponding total configurations: $N_\Lambda = \exp(10^{124})$. This is the current "going rate" for explaining our Universe's initial conditions.

**The question**: Are we done counting? What about the information content of life?

## Why Biology Lies Outside the Newtonian Paradigm (Sections 3–5)

### The Newtonian Paradigm

The standard entropy calculation rests on four assumptions:
1. Laws are (quantum) deterministic
2. Dynamical laws distinguish trajectories in configuration space
3. Laws remain unchanged throughout evolution
4. **Configuration space is fixed** — does not itself change during evolution

This is the Newtonian Paradigm. The crucial assumption: the last one.

### Ever-Expanding Configuration Space

In biology, useful configuration spaces continually evolve in time. A fish develops a swim bladder; a bacterium finds it and creates an unprecedented bound state. This could not have been predicted, nor written as a law. The biological universe is highly *non-ergodic*: almost none of the possible states are ever occupied.

**The one rule that applies to all living systems without exception**: "The name of the game is getting to exist." Any more specific rule fails in some circumstances.

### The Missing Standard Model of Biology

In physics, successive EFTs (effective field theories) emerge at lower temperatures — nuclei at $T_{\text{nucleo}}$, atoms at lower $T$, etc. But all can be derived from the Standard Model's single, fixed, time-independent Hilbert space.

In biology, similar emergent bound states arise (complex organics → amino acids → proteins → organelles → proto-cells → cells → eukaryotes → multicellular organisms → ...) with genuinely new effective interactions at each level. But there is **no underlying standard model of biology** from which to derive them. The configuration space is always growing in unpredictable directions.

**Central claim**: If new effective interactions and their associated novel bound states are continuously emerging, the associated Hilbert space is ever growing, and we are never finished counting possible states.

### Emergent vs. Fundamental Degrees of Freedom

The paper argues that emergent degrees of freedom must be counted as equivalent to fundamental ones for thermodynamic purposes. This is standard in condensed matter physics (superconductivity, quantum materials) and must apply to biology. The biological configuration space generated by emergent functional degrees of freedom *vastly dominates* the space generated by fundamental degrees of freedom — especially in regimes of extreme non-ergodicity and exceptional organizational complexity.

**Key example**: Life on Earth uses ~20 amino acids, giving $20^{200} \approx 10^{260}$ possible chains of length 200. Almost none are occupied — only those produced by long specific sequences of catalyzed reactions granting functional evolutionary advantage. The system is far from thermal equilibrium.

**Critical distinction**: We can define the *physical* entropy of the biosphere but cannot define its *functional* entropy (the number of possible functions is indeﬁnite). Yet it is precisely the functions of proteins that account for their existence.

## Permutational Microstates: Three Thought Experiments (Section 7)

### Thought Experiment A: Simplified Cell Model

- 10³ genes × 10³ triplets = 10⁶ amino acids per genome
- $N_{\text{genomes}} = 20^{10^6} \approx 10^{10^6}$ possible genomes
- $N_{\text{imaginable biospheres}} = 2^{N_{\text{genomes}}} \approx 10^{10^{10^6}}$ — far exceeds $N_\Lambda$
- But these are *imaginable*, not *realizable*: most contain too many species to construct from all matter in the Universe
- Resource-limited version (10⁷ species): $N_{\text{realizable}} \approx 10^{10^{13}}$ — well within $N_\Lambda$

### Thought Experiment B: Multicellular Biospheres

- Gene expression creates $2^{1000} \approx 10^{300}$ possible cell types per genome
- Including populations and species diversity: $N_{\text{multicell biospheres}} \approx 10^{3 \times 10^{27}}$
- Still within $N_\Lambda$ — exponent essentially given by total number of cells (resource-limited)

### Thought Experiment C: Lego Star Wars

- $N$ pieces with $M$ ways to snap them together (all degenerate with ground state)
- The degenerate Hilbert space has dimension $M \times d_{\text{toy}}$ — larger than the naive Hilbert space by factor $M$
- **Principle of opacity**: Once object 3 is created from 1 and 2, it "forgets" its origin and can recombine with 1 or 2 to create further novel items. This opacity enables super-combinatorial growth.

### The Discovered Pattern

$$N_{\text{resource-limited}} \ll N_\Lambda \ll N_{\text{comprehensive}}$$

Comprehensive counts (all possibilities) vastly exceed $N_\Lambda$; resource-limited counts are dwarfed by it. Permutational operations alone do not suffice to exceed the holographic bound — only combinatorial innovation (TAP) can compete.

### Principles of Hierarchical Construction

The key mechanism: starting from $M_0$ parts, subsets give $M_1 = 2^{M_0}$ composites. Subsets of subsets: $M_2 = 2^{M_1} = 2^{2^{M_0}}$. At each level, the number of composite objects exceeds the number of original parts.

**Opacity principle**: Once parts combine, the composite "forgets" internal structure and can recombine freely, treated as an independent entity in the effective theory.

## The Theory of the Adjacent Possible and the TAP Equation (Section 8)

At any moment in biological evolution, the next step cannot be prestated because the underlying laws cannot be predicted. But what happens next is limited by what already exists — evolution expands through neighboring possibilities enabled by combinations of existing items.

The TAP equation:

$$M_{t+1} = M_t(1 - \mu) + \sum_{i=2}^{M_t} \alpha_i \binom{M_t}{i}$$

Power-law variant with $\alpha_i = \alpha / a^{i-1}$:

$$M_{t+1} = M_t(1 - \mu) + \alpha a \left[\left(1 + \frac{1}{a}\right)^{M_t} - \frac{M_t}{a} - 1\right]$$

Behavior: slow growth while $t < \alpha^{-1}$, then once $\alpha 2^{M_t} \sim M_t$ the combinatoric term dominates and super-exponential explosion occurs.

## Application: The Road to Organic Complexity (Section 9)

### Setup

- **Macrostates**: "Alive" (contains at least one living organism) vs. "Dead" (conventional cosmology)
- **Microstates**: All possible biospheres that could have evolved from same initial conditions
- **Building blocks**: CHNOPS (Carbon, Hydrogen, Nitrogen, Oxygen, Phosphorus, Sulphur) → $M_0 = 6$

### Parameter Choices

- $\alpha = 0.1$ (degenerate with timestep; each step ≈ 10⁵ years)
- $a = 250$ (suppresses multi-element combinations beyond ~250 amino acid length)
- $\mu = 10^{-4}$ (background extinction rate; sub-critical)
- Evolution from onset of life ($t_{\text{OL}} = 3.7$ Gya) to template synthesis ($t_{\text{TS}} = 3.5$ Gya)

### Result

The characteristic hockey-stick behavior: slow growth followed by super-exponential blow-up.

$$N_\Lambda(t_{\text{TS}}) \sim 10^{10^{120}} \ll N_{\text{Bio}}(t_{\text{TS}}) \sim 10^{10^{237}}$$

**By the time of RNA template synthesis (3.5 billion years ago), the biosphere's configuration space already vastly exceeded the entire Universe's vacuum entropy.**

And it has continued to expand ever since, punctuated by mass extinction events but generically growing.

## Biological Dark Energy (Section 10)

A speculative connection: RNA (common ancestor of all life) first appeared ~3.5 Gya, which is redshift $z \sim 0.3$ — the same epoch when dark energy came to dominate the cosmic energy budget (matter-dark energy equality at $z \sim 0.30$).

Could the emergence of life have caused the emergence of dark energy? Objections noted (dark energy effects visible before Earth formed; density should be growing not constant; unclear mechanism for one location to affect the whole Universe). But the coincidence merits exploration.

## Key Conclusions

1. **In physics**: the Hilbert space does not expand. All states existed from the beginning; their availability depends only on temperature regime. Everything derivable from the Standard Model.

2. **In biology**: the configuration space genuinely and unpredictably expands in real time. Novel bound states emerge that could not have been derived a priori from any underlying theory.

3. **The creative potential Q**: In the Newtonian paradigm, $Q = 1$ (all information in initial conditions). In an evolving-laws paradigm, $Q \gg 1$ — the Universe creates information. A biological universe can create more information than was present at the Big Bang.

4. **TAP operates at an abstract level**: It quantifies the *remembered ability* of a system to make and remake objects. The word "remembered" is crucial — existence must be encoded (in RNA, folklore, patents). To keep making leopards requires the entire sustainable ecosystem.

5. **This does NOT worsen the initial conditions problem**: The vast numbers generated by biospheres are due to state space growth enabled by emergent complexity. Those states did not exist until life created them. The newborn Universe had no opportunity to occupy them.

## Key Concepts for sigma-TAP Integration

1. **The TAP equation parameters are biologically motivated**: $M_0 = 6$ (CHNOPS), $\alpha = 0.1$ (timescale), $a = 250$ (protein length suppression), $\mu = 10^{-4}$ (background extinction). sigma-TAP uses different values ($\alpha = 5 \times 10^{-3}$, $a = 3.0$ or $8.0$, $\mu = 0.005$) calibrated for agent simulation rather than biochemistry.

2. **Opacity = the structural basis for combinatorial innovation**: Once a composite forms, it forgets its internal structure and can recombine freely. This is why TAP produces super-combinatorial growth. In sigma-TAP, the type_set serves this role — agents are characterized by their type_set (what they can do), not by the history of how those types were acquired.

3. **Resource limitation vs. comprehensive possibility**: $N_{\text{resource-limited}} \ll N_\Lambda \ll N_{\text{comprehensive}}$. The sigma-TAP simulation operates in the resource-limited regime (finite agents, finite types) but models processes that in principle have unbounded comprehensive possibility.

4. **Non-ergodicity is structural**: The biosphere occupies a vanishingly sparse subset of its possible states. This is not a deficiency but a defining characteristic. sigma-TAP agents similarly explore only a tiny fraction of possible type configurations.

5. **Emergent degrees of freedom counted as fundamental**: This justifies modeling emergent agent-level properties (TAPS signatures, L-matrix profiles, trust metrics) as fundamental variables rather than derived quantities. The paper explicitly argues that condensed-matter and biological emergence requires treating emergent DOF equivalently.

6. **"The name of the game is getting to exist"**: The only biological law that applies universally. In sigma-TAP terms: the only thing that matters is whether an agent persists — time as praxitive deferral of consummation.

7. **The missing standard model of biology = why sigma-TAP cannot be purely deterministic**: There is no fixed law from which to derive the next step. The simulation must incorporate genuine unpredictability (stochastic elements, seed entropy) because the target phenomenon is inherently non-derivable.

8. **TAP as remembered ability**: Not physical collisions but the encoded capacity to recreate. Maps directly to the practico-inert — sedimented past praxis that stores the potential energy for future creation.
